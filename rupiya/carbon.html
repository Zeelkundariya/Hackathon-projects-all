What is Carbon Credit?

India holds immense potential for driving sustainable development and combating climate change.

Carbon credits are a key component of emissions trading that aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. In simple terms, one carbon credit is equivalent to the reduction of one ton of carbon dioxide (CO2) or its equivalent in other greenhouse gases. These credits are tradable commodities and serve as a way to incentivize and finance emission reduction projects.

Key Principles of Regenerative Farming

Soil Health First

Focus on building soil organic matter through cover crops and minimal tillage.

Crop Rotation

Diversify crops to break pest cycles and improve nutrient cycling.

Integrate Livestock

Use animals to naturally fertilize and manage land sustainably.

Water Management

Improve water infiltration and reduce runoff through better soil structure.

Cover Cropping

Keep soil covered year-round to prevent erosion and feed soil biology.

No Synthetic Inputs

Minimize chemical fertilizers and pesticides for healthier ecosystems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is regenerative agriculture? +

Regenerative agriculture is a farming approach that focuses on rehabilitating and enhancing the entire ecosystem of the farm by placing soil health at the center of all practices.

How do I earn carbon credits? +

You earn carbon credits by implementing verified regenerative practices that sequester carbon in your soil. We measure and certify your carbon sequestration efforts.

How does carbon sequestration work? +

Plants absorb CO2 from the atmosphere through photosynthesis. In regenerative systems, this carbon is stored in the soil as organic matter, effectively removing it from the atmosphere.

Is this difficult to start? +

We provide comprehensive support including training, resources, and ongoing guidance to make the transition to regenerative agriculture as smooth as possible.

What are the main benefits for farmers? +

Benefits include improved soil health, reduced input costs, additional income from carbon credits, increased resilience to climate extremes, and long-term sustainability.
